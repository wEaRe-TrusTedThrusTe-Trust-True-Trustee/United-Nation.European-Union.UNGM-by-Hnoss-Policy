## 5. ETHICAL GUIDELINES

### 5.1 Impartiality Standards

The Foundation commits to:

- Neutral arbitrator selection processes
- Absence of institutional bias
- Disclosure of conflicts of interest
- Equal access to dispute resolution mechanisms

### 5.2 Confidentiality Protocol

All arbitration proceedings are subject to:

- Default confidentiality (unless parties agree otherwise)
- Non-disclosure of internal deliberations
- Secure document handling (encrypted channels)
- Limited publication of awards (anonymized when required)

### 5.3 Sustainability & Accessibility

- Promote virtual hearings to reduce carbon footprint
- Offer tiered fee structures for SME access
- Support pro bono arbitration for humanitarian cases
- Invest in arbitration capacity building in developing regions
